Matt Hodges

Work

I am currently the Head of Technology and Incubation at Zinc Collective where I lead pilot programs and technical partnerships to address infrastrucurte gaps in the Democratic political ecosystem.

I sit on the Advisory Board of Higher Ground Labs, an accelerator that invests in early-stage political tech companies and convenes the creators and users of innovative tech.

I was the Director of Engineering for President Joe Biden on the 2020 campaign where I lead a team of software and infrastructure engineers that powered the campaign that defeated Donald Trump [TKTKTKKT say more here].

I was a Senior Software Engineer on The Groundwork platform, which served as the application and infrastrucutre system that powered the 2016 presidential campaign of Secretary Hillary Clinton.

I was an Infrastructure Engineer at Datadog where I architected and implemented the internal platform for Incident Response management, which was eventually developed into the suite of customer product offerings.

I was a Senior Mobile Engineer at LISNR, where I developed the product's first iOS SDK for an ultrasonic data transport protocol.

I was a Senior Mobile Engineer and Scrum Master at Cardinal Solutions, with a team that built Vantiv Accept - a mobile credit card processing application.

I am a Named Inventor on an Augmented Reality Patent Application (US20140092241A1) for a "device and method for scanning of books and other items for order and inventory control".

I maintain an Artificial Intelligence Twitter bot called Campaign Email AI that was built on a text-generating neural network. It was trained on hundreds of thousands of real campaign emails from the Princeton Corpus of Political Emails, and attempts to generate new campaign email subject lines. It serves as both an experiment and an art project to demostrate the shortcomings of AI-driven voter outreach.

I have been an Election Night Reporter for Decision Desk HQ. [TKTKTK]

I have been a frequent Contributor to OpenElections. [TKTKTK]
